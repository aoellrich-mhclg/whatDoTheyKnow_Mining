In order to ensure that we have interpreted your request in the correct
way we would like to ask you for clarification. You have asked about
statutory overrides. We implement statutory overrides through legislation
which permits or ensures that relevant local authorities implement the
specific statutory override. There are also occasions where local
authorities make capitalisation requests to the department, these are
requests to treat revenue expenditure as capital which overrides standard
accounting practices. We would like to check if you are requesting
information about the former, statutory overrides, or capitalisation
requests or both?
 
I will not be able to take this matter further without this extra
information from you. Please let me know by 09/05/2019.
 
If I do not hear from you within the timeframe provided, I shall take it
that you do not wish to pursue this request and will consider the request
closed. 
 
Yours sincerely
 
 
Ashwin Jolly
Deputy FOI Business Partner

Dear Despatch Box,

In relation to question 4) Your response was as follows:

"The Secretary of State can also override normal accounting practices which determine what expenditure is categorised as capital or revenue. This is done using the vires in Section 21 of the Local Government Act to insert provisions into the Capital Finance and Accounting (England) (Regulations) 2003. These statutory overrides are not managed on a case by case basis. Instead, an override can be implemented that applies equally to the whole sector. These overrides can be
viewed by looking at the Capital Finance and Accounting Regulations 2003"

This sounds very much like a statutory override, for example the overrides granted by MHCLG in relation to IFRS9, in November/December 2018.

Can you please clarify why the granting of statutory overrides in relation to IFRS9, within the scope of Q4 of this FOIA request was not clearly listed as such within your FOI response?

An example of the application of MHCLG's override in relation to IFRS appears to be seen below in the published accounts of Sheffield Council.

Pages 49,50 of Sheffield's published 2017/18 annual accounts, as approved by external auditor Mr Tim Cutler of KPMG states as follows: https://www.sheffield.gov.uk/content/dam...

"IFRS 9 Financial Instruments
IFRS 9 Financial Instruments has been implemented in the 2018/19 CIPFA Accounting
Code of Practice. Its introduction will see the classifications of financial assets change to
Amortised Cost, Fair Value through Comprehensive Income and Fair Value through Profit
and Loss, from the previous categories of Loans and Receivables, Available for Sale and
Fair Value through Profit and Loss.

The second main change will be the introduction of an expected credit loss model for
particular asset types, rather than an impairment of the asset resulting from a specific
incident. ***Certain aspects of the introduction of IFRS 9 have been adapted for local
authorities, effectively removing the IFRS 9 implications. These cover such areas as soft
loans, Lender Option Borrowing Option (LOBO) loans, immaterial transactions,
exchanges of debt instruments and hedge accounting."***

Can you please clarify/ confirm if this process of "adapting IFRS9 for local authorities, effectively removing IFRS9 implications" is generally known in local government circles as a statutory override?

And if so, why these overrides were not listed by MHCLG for the financial years requested in relation to your answer to Q4

Yours sincerely,

Joel M Benjamin

Despatch Box, Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government 28 February 2019

Dear Mr Benjamin
 
Many thanks for your email of 22nd Feburary 2019 asking for
clarification, which we are very happy to provide here.
 
Our previous response referred to an 'override to normal accounting
practices'. This could be interpreted as a 'statutory override' by those
in the sector. The override to normal accounting practices that requires
local authorities to reverse out some of the fair value gains and losses
incurred by IFRS 9 for a time limited period, is done through vires in
Section 21 of the Local Government Act to insert provisions into the Local
Authority (Capital Finance and Accounting) (England) (Regulations) 2003
(the '2003 regulations). All local authorities must adhere to overrides of
this nature in their accounting practices. Our previous response did not
list all individual overrides to normal accounting practices or the local
authorities that have applied them. This is because we do not collect data
on which authorities are using the override. It is the responsibility of
individual local authorities to ensure that their accounting practices are
compliant with the relevant standards and legislation. Our response
indicated that all overrides of this nature can be viewed by looking at
the 2003 regulations.

The specific override to normal accounting practices relating to IFRS 9
requires local authorities to report the unrealised fair value movements
resulting from pooled investment funds in a sperate unusable reserve. This
was done to ensure that there was an adequate level of transparency in
local authority accounts throughout the duration of the override.

I trust that this provides the clarification you require.

Yours sincerely,

Knowledge and Information Access Team
